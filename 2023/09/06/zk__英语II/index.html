In addition, they provide their customers with great discounts on products. With this in mind, it is clear to see why so many shoppers buy many of their Christmas gifts on Black Friday.
Black Friday is not as old as many people think. In fact, it is believed that the first Black Friday was held in the 1970s in the USA. It was a day when stores decided to mark the start of the holiday season. In order to draw more customers, they offered great discounts. All products sold very well. They hope to attract shoppers into their stores.
Black Friday is a day when many shoppers in the USA go out and buy gifts, even though Christmas Eve is still a more popular day to shop. Thanks to the Internet, it is now possible to get some excellent Black Friday deals online. （一）黑色星期五

每个人都喜欢在节日期间购买礼物，但很少有人知道节日购物的历史。虽然人们听说过黑色星期五，但大多数人不知道它的起源。黑色星期五是感恩节后的第一天。在这一天，大多数零售商店很早就开门——有的甚至凌晨4点。此外，他们在产品上为顾客提供很大的折扣。考虑到这一点，就很清楚为什么那么多购物者在黑色星期五购买很多圣诞礼物了。
“黑色星期五”并没有很多人想象的那么古老。事实上，人们认为第一个黑色星期五是20世纪70年代在美国举行的。这是商店决定标志节日季开始的一天。为了吸引更多的顾客，他们提供很大的折扣。所有产品都很畅销。这一巨大的成功造就了“黑色星期五”的名字。之所以叫这个名字，是因为这些商店“有盈余”。这个财务术语指的是商店赚了很多钱。然而，直到2002年左右，黑色星期五才真正开始流行起来。今天在美国，无数的广告商自豪地宣布他们的黑色星期五销售。他们希望吸引购物者到他们的商店。
黑色星期五是美国许多购物者外出购买礼物的一天，尽管平安夜仍然是更受欢迎的购物日。多亏了互联网，现在有可能在网上实现一些很好的黑色星期五交易。所以如果你不想在凌晨4点前赶到商店，这是购买减价商品的最佳方式。

（二）Farms Appear in Cities

　　When you hear the word “farm”, you may imagine the countryside with cows and crops. But scientists say the farms of the future could be built in tall buildings in some large cities.
　　It may be difficult to believe, but in fact, the technology for growing crops in doors already exists. Farming in the city is already happening. Some vegetables are already grown in greenhouses. Even the scientists at the South Pole research station can enjoy fresh vegetables they grow in their own greenhouse.
　　Experts say indoor farming solves many problems. First, traditional farming takes up a lot of land. Growing crops in tall buildings — called vertical farming（垂直农业）— can solve the problem. Also, fruits and vegetables grown indoors would not face serious threats from insects and weather.
　　Though vertical farms don’t exist yet, experts have created a plan for recycling in such farms. The water from indoor fish ponds would be used to water crops. Gases from crop waste would be used to heat the building. Waste from chicken or pigs would be reused as a source of energy.
　　But experts believe vertical farming is not going to be easy. They say it can be difficult to control climate conditions indoors. Besides, Plants differ in their weather and lighting needs. For example, some plants like warm, sunny weather, but other plants prefer cooler temperatures.
That’s not to say these difficulties won’t be overcome — but it will take time. Most experts suggest it may take about 5 to 15 years before the first vertical farms could be created.

（二）城市里的农场

当你听到“farm”这个词时，你可能会想象有奶牛和庄稼的乡村。但科学家表示，未来的农场可以建在一些大城市的高层建筑中。

虽然垂直农场还不存在，但专家已经制定了一个在这样的农场中回收利用的计划。室内鱼塘的水将用于灌溉作物。来自农作物废料的气体将被用来为大楼供暖。鸡或猪的粪便将被重新利用作为能源。
但专家认为，垂直农业并不容易。他们说，很难控制室内的气候条件。此外，植物的气候和照明需求也各不相同。例如，一些植物喜欢温暖、阳光充足的天气，而另一些植物则喜欢凉爽的温度。
这并不是说这些困难不会被克服，而是需要时间。大多数专家认为，第一个垂直农场可能需要大约5到15年的时间才能建成。

（一）

你可能想知道为什么超市都一样。这并不是因为运营它们的公司缺乏想象力。这是因为他们都是为了说服人们买东西。
在超市里，人们需要一段时间才能进入购物模式。这就是为什么紧邻入口的区域被称为“减压区”的原因。人们需要放慢脚步，环顾四周，即使他们是常客。就销售而言，这一领域有点亏损，因此往往更多地用于促销。
购物者可能会立即进入新鲜水果和蔬菜区。对于购物者来说，这毫无意义。水果和蔬菜很容易损坏，所以应该在购物之旅结束时而不是开始时购买。但这里起作用的是什么？事实证明，选择好的新鲜食物是开始购物的一种方式，它可以让人们对以后吃不健康的东西感到不那么内疚。
购物者已经知道，日常用品，如牛奶，总是放在商店后面，以提供更多的机会吸引顾客。但是超市知道购物者知道这一点，所以他们会使用其他技巧，比如把受欢迎的商品放在货架的中间，这样人们就不得不沿着货架走去寻找它们。这个想法是为了增加“停留时间”：人们在商店里花费的时间长度。
传统零售商衡量“足球”，因为进入商店的人数是已知的，但这些数字并没有说明人们去哪里以及他们在那里花了多长时间。但如今，有一项技术可以填补这一空白：手机。Path Intelligence是一家英国公司，它在朴茨茅斯的一家大型零售中心Gunharf Quays跟踪人们的手机，不是通过监控通话，而是通过在手机自动传输到蜂窝网络时绘制手机位置。研究发现，当停留时间增加1%时，销售额增加1.3%。
由于对购物者如何做出选择的理解加深，这种技术越来越受欢迎。人们告诉市场研究人员，他们会考虑价格、选择或便利性等因素，对买什么做出理性决定。但潜意识的力量，包括情感和记忆，显然也在起作用。

（二）Kindness of Strangers

At the store where I worked, I noticed a young boy of about ten shopping alone with his school list. He placed everything in his basket and went to the checkout.
The young shop assistant told him the total price, $37.60. The boy was instantly disappointed and said he had only been given $20. He took out one thing at a time until he got down to his $20. With eyes full of tears, he asked if he could phone his mum to pick him up. When he got through, it appeared that even this was inconvenient for his mother.
He bit hard on his lip to stop the tears and walked outside to wait on the bench in front of the shop. This was all too much for the young assistant who had served him and she said, “I wish I could afford to pay for the rest of his things.” One of the other girls said she had a few dollars. Then two others said they could help as well.
These four young girls pooled their money and found that they had more than enough money for the boy’s extra purchases. So they upgraded his pens and pencils to top-quality ones, then took the bag out to the boy on the seat. His young face changed from sadness to the most beautiful smile. His mother arrived much later to pick him up. Instead of running to the car, he ran back into the store and called out, “Thank you!”
I was so proud of my team that day. They did something wonderful for that boy and also found the pleasure of giving was as great as receiving.

在迷信的年代和国家，梦是非常被重视的。每个梦都被认为是有意义的，为人们解梦是牧师和占星家的工作。它们通常被视为对未来的预测，对即将到来的危险或悲伤的警告，或对即将到来的好运的预言。梦被认为是来自神灵或死者灵魂的超自然交流，因此是神圣的启示。受过教育的人不再以这种方式看待梦。有人嘲笑它们只是幻想，不值得认真考虑；但也有人觉得它们很有趣，可以作为心理学研究。
一方面，梦证明我们在睡眠中并不是完全无意识的。我们大脑的一部分是清醒的并在工作。在睡眠中，我们意识不到周围的环境。我们什么也看不见，什么也听不到，对我们周围发生的事一无所知。然而，我们做梦的事实证明我们并不是完全无意识的。试图发现梦的原因通常是很有趣的。有些梦纯粹是生理的原因。一个人梦见他走在火山口附近，他的脚被滚烫的岩石烫伤了；但当他醒来时，他发现自己的脚压在了热水壶上。
大多数梦都是我们生活中过去事件的混乱和不连贯的记忆。我们经常可以追溯一个梦，发现它是由我们一两天前看到的、听到的或读到的东西所暗示的。例如，一位律师深夜一直在思考一个案件，上床睡觉时除了蜥蜴什么也没梦到。早晨，当他回到书房时，他发现自己在考虑这件事的时候，一直在不自觉地盯着他的钟，钟的表壳上装饰着一只蜥蜴的铜像。梦指的是过去，而不是未来。

Media and Shopping
　　With numerous technological advances, various kinds of media have been well developed. As a result, an increasing number of people are fond of purchasing goods with the help of media, such as newspapers, radio stations, TV stations and the Internet. 　　As we know, every coin has two sides and shopping via media is no exception. On the one hand, it saves much time, money and effort to purchase goods through media since you don’t have to go to the shops in person. Besides, you can choose what you want from an enormous variety of goods on display. On the other hand, you may be cheated sometimes by the false information and suffer a considerable loss. As far as I am concerned, shopping via media brings great benefits to our life and our economy on the whole. At the same time, we need strengthened supervision over the information released via media. In this way, we can reap more benefits from shopping via media and reduce the possible harm to a minimum.
